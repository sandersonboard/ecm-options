Optro ECM — Evidence sufficiency agent

Helios Robotics, Inc. · SOX 404 program · FY2026
Version v0.3.2 In production 2026-07-01 Last review 2026-09-15 by S. Park (PMO) Next review 2026-12-31 (Q4 close)

Intended use

ECM pre-judges the structural sufficiency of SOX evidence at submission time. It checks that artifacts cover the required period, carry the required fields, and match the schema declared by the control's evidence template (see aiready-b). When a structural gap is detected, ECM blocks submission with a specific diagnostic; the owner sees what's missing before the package reaches the reviewer.

ECM is assistive. It does not author the substantive verdict, it does not attest on behalf of any party, and it does not bypass the reviewer. The reviewer's judgment and signature remain authoritative.

What ECM does and doesn't do

✓ ECM does
  • Parse submitted artifacts against the control's schema
  • Surface structural gaps at submission time
  • Retrieve carryover findings from prior sign-offs (verbatim)
  • Tag events from connected source systems to the controls they touch
  • Maintain provenance graph for every artifact
  • Propose evidence drafts based on prior-cycle patterns
✗ ECM doesn't
  • Author the substantive sufficiency verdict
  • Attest to anything on behalf of management
  • Auto-accept any evidence — every submission requires a human action
  • Compose the reviewer's finding language
  • Make causal inferences without explicit human ratification
  • Hide its reasoning behind opaque model weights (see policy-as-code)

Known limitations

  • Substantive sufficiency varies by control. ECM's structural checks generalize across the control catalog; the substantive judgment doesn't and is reviewer-owned.
  • NL parsing has error modes. Conversational intake (Slack-C, Mobile-C, palette-B) requires explicit owner confirmation of the parsed structured action before filing.
  • Auto-attestation is policy-bounded. Only controls explicitly listed in POL-AT-2026 can be filed under standing approval (see policy-as-code).
  • Connector classifications are reversible. Any auto-tag can be overridden by the owner or reviewer, and overrides feed the training set for future tuning.

Evaluation

Agreement with human reviewers, measured quarterly over a 6-quarter rolling window: structural checks 96.8% · substantive matches 81.3% · finding attribution 74.1%. Substantive disagreement drives the training signal for the policy-as-code rule updates (see Take C).
