! Verification failed — chain integrity broken

One block's recomputed hash does not match the chain's recorded root. That means either the artifact, the metadata, or one of the signatures has been altered since the sign-off was committed. This is the surface ECM is built to detect — and the verifier behaves correctly by refusing to issue a green certificate.

What to do next.
  1. Audit committee is auto-notified. External auditor's working paper widget will refuse the cite.
  2. Optro pulls the divergent block's audit log to identify the actor + timestamp of the edit.
  3. Marie's original sign-off package is reconstructed from the immutable ledger snapshot (not the local store).
  4. Ada opens a deficiency review under HEL-DEF-2026-04 with full chain history attached.

When it fails. The verifier doesn't "pass with a warning" — it refuses the certificate entirely. The system is built so this surface only ever fires when something has genuinely happened, and when it fires, the path to resolution starts with auto-notification, not with a human noticing later. The point of the chain isn't that nothing ever goes wrong; it's that when something does, the evidence is in the ledger, not in someone's testimony.

ECM writes the chain narrative in plain English so a CFO or audit-committee chair can read it without learning blockchain vocabulary. The cryptography is real; the page in front of the executive is a paragraph and a list.
