Problem

The cost was never the upload.

The way SOX evidence gets collected today assumes the control owner will go into a web app to upload artifacts, the reviewer will log in to judge them, and the PMO will check a dashboard to see where things stand.

The people doing this mostly do not work in audit, so the app is the part they put off. And the real cost was never the upload. When evidence comes back incomplete or wrong, someone has to work out what is missing before they can even ask for a corrected version, then chase the owner until it shows up. Multiply that across every control and every quarter and you have the actual job.

Solution

Move the judgment, not just the form.

The slow part of collection was always the judgment, not the file transfer. Someone has to decide whether the evidence covers the right period, carries the fields it needs, and actually shows the control operated. That decision is the thing an agent can now make. ECM reads what gets submitted, compares it against what the control requires, and surfaces the gaps before a reviewer has to find them by hand.

Once the agent carries that judgment, the work stops needing a fixed home in a web app. It can run wherever the owner and reviewer already are.

Four rules hold on every surface. Never accept evidence automatically · Always leave an escape hatch for a person · Every AI judgment is legible · The trace is the output.
